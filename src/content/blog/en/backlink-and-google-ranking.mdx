## What Is a Backlink and How Does Google Evaluate It?

A backlink is a hyperlink from another website pointing to yours. For example, if a news portal links to your article — that counts as a backlink to you. In Google's algorithms, backlinks function as "votes": the more quality sites link to you, the more trustworthy and useful Google considers your site.

But here's the key nuance: **not all backlinks are equal.** Google evaluates each link using several criteria:

- **Authority of the linking site (Domain Authority).** A single backlink from a DR 80 site has more impact than 50 backlinks from DR 10 sites. Domain authority is a metric showing a site's overall trustworthiness and power, measured by SEO tools like Ahrefs and Moz.
- **Link context and relevance.** If you blog about technology and get a link from a cooking recipe site — Google considers this link less valuable. Topical relevance makes backlinks stronger.
- **Dofollow or nofollow.** Dofollow links tell Google "I trust this site, I'm vouching for it." Nofollow doesn't pass this signal. Most forum and comment links are nofollow.
- **Anchor text — the link text.** Instead of "click here," an anchor like "SEO services in Uzbekistan" signals Google about what your site covers.

Google's PageRank algorithm was built on backlinks. While it's become much more complex today, the core principle hasn't changed: **quality backlinks = higher positions.** The 2024 Google Leak documents confirmed this — showing that backlink profiles remain one of the most important ranking factors.

## How Domain Age Affects Backlinks — Does Google Trust New Sites?

The first question many new site owners have: "My site is new — will Google trust me?" This is a very valid question because there's a direct relationship between domain age and backlink effectiveness.

**Google doesn't officially acknowledge the "sandbox" effect**, but in practice, new domains face noticeable limitations in organic traffic during the first 3–6 months. This means you can write perfect content and acquire backlinks, but Google still views your site as being in a "trial period." Is domain age important for SEO? **Yes, but it's not the only factor.**

Here's how domain age affects backlink effectiveness:

| Domain Age | Backlink Effectiveness | Google Trust Level |
|---|---|---|
| 0–3 months | Low — links get indexed but ranking impact is minimal | Very low — sandbox period |
| 3–6 months | Medium — Google starts monitoring the site | Low-medium |
| 6–12 months | Good — backlinks start showing noticeable results | Medium |
| 1–2 years | High — each quality backlink has strong impact | High |
| 3+ years | Maximum — site has established full trust | Very high |

In our projects, we've observed this pattern: new websites built by the Katov team showed minimal organic traffic in the first 3 months, but after the backlink strategy kicked in at 6 months, traffic grew 3x on average.

So, **a new site should be at least 3–6 months old for backlinks** to be fully counted by Google. But this doesn't mean you should sit and wait 6 months — on the contrary, starting your backlink strategy from day one while expecting results in 6–12 months is the right approach.

The advantage of older domains is that they're already in Google's index, have accumulated history, and earned a certain level of trust. **Older domain backlink benefits** can be 3–5x higher compared to new domains — especially if the domain has been active throughout.

## Which Backlinks Improve Google Ranking — Quality vs. Quantity

"More backlinks = higher ranking" is one of the biggest misconceptions in SEO. Since Google's 2012 Penguin update, the search engine has been fighting against low-quality, spam backlinks. Today, a single quality backlink delivers more results than dozens of low-quality links.

**Signs of a quality backlink:**

1. **Comes from trusted, authoritative sites.** A link from a major news portal or a well-known industry blog is a powerful signal.
2. **Placed naturally within content.** Not in footers, sidebars, or comments — but within the article body, contextually relevant.
3. **From a relevant site.** If you provide IT services, backlinks from technology or business sites are most effective. For example, a link from a technology portal to an article about [website creation services](/blog/website-creation-services) — that's ideal.
4. **Is dofollow.** This directly passes ranking signals to Google.
5. **Comes from diverse domains.** Getting one link each from 100 different sites is better than 100 links from one site.

**Low-quality (harmful) backlinks:**

- Purchased links (link farms, PBNs)
- Technically generated spam links
- Mass directory submissions
- Links from topically unrelated sites
- Many links with identical anchor text (over-optimization)

Google uses its SpamBrain algorithm to detect such links. If your backlink profile looks unnatural — there's a risk of manual penalty or algorithmic demotion. **Google's backlink guidelines** are clear: natural links that provide value to users are the only right path.

## Backlink Strategy for New Sites — Step by Step

In a new site's SEO strategy, acquiring backlinks is the hardest but most important step. Because a new domain is unknown, has limited content, and a small audience. Here's a practical, step-by-step strategy:

### Step 1: Build a Strong Content Base (1–2 months)

Before acquiring backlinks, you need link-worthy content. Nobody links to empty or low-quality sites. Write at least 10–15 deep, valuable articles:

- **Research or statistics** — original data becomes a source for other sites
- **Complete guides** — step-by-step explanations in long-form articles
- **Comparative analyses** — "A vs B" format content attracts many links

### Step 2: Register in Local and Industry Directories (2–3 months)

- Create a Google Business Profile
- Register in local business catalogs
- Join relevant industry directories
- Useful platforms for Uzbekistan: Google Business Profile, Yellowpages.uz, Yandex Spravochnik, Clutch.co for IT companies, GitHub, ProductHunt.
- These backlinks are weak but provide "existence signals" to Google

### Step 3: Guest Posting and Relationship Building (3–6 months)

- Write guest articles for blogs in your niche
- Build relationships with journalists and bloggers
- Use platforms like HARO (Help a Reporter Out)
- Each guest post = one new quality backlink and new audience

### Step 4: Broken Link Building and Resource Pages (ongoing)

- Find backlinks pointing to competitors' 404 pages (using Ahrefs or SEMrush)
- Contact site owners: "This link is broken, consider linking to my content instead"
- Suggest your site for resource pages ("Useful links" sections)

### Step 5: Content Marketing and Natural Backlink Earning (6+ months)

Over time, if your content is truly valuable, other sites will naturally link to you. This is the ideal scenario — Google considers this the most natural and trustworthy signal. Offer free tools on your site — for example, an [interactive website price calculator](/studio/price) is very effective for earning natural backlinks.

## Backlink Trust and Domain Authority — How Google Measures Trust

Google assigns "trust scores" to websites — while not officially announced, SEO professionals measure this through various metrics. The two most popular: **Domain Authority (DA)** by Moz and **Domain Rating (DR)** by Ahrefs. These metrics are based on a site's backlink profile strength.

**How does domain authority increase?**

- Acquiring backlinks from quality, trusted sites
- Building a stable backlink profile over time
- Absence of spam links and toxic backlinks
- Technical site health (fast loading, SSL, mobile responsiveness) — we've written about this in detail on our [website services](/service/website) page
- Consistently updated, useful content

**Backlink trust** indicates how much Google trusts your links. If you receive links from high-trust sites (.gov, .edu, major media) — your trust score increases too. This works on the "trust chain" principle: when a trusted site links to you, Google begins to view you as trustworthy.

In practice, this means:

| Metric | New Site (0–6 months) | Mid-range Site (6–24 months) | Strong Site (2+ years) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Domain Authority | 1–10 | 10–30 | 30–60+ |
| Backlink Count (approx.) | 0–50 | 50–500 | 500–5,000+ |
| Google Trust | Low | Medium | High |
| Ranking Speed | Slow (weeks) | Medium (days) | Fast (hours) |

Important note: **domain authority cannot be artificially inflated.** Attempting to boost DA with spam links will likely result in a Google penalty. The only right path — patiently building quality backlinks.

## When to Start Backlinks — The Most Common Mistakes

The biggest mistake new site owners make is postponing backlinks for "later." The second mistake is immediately buying hundreds of backlinks. Both approaches are wrong. The right answer: **start backlinks from day one, but gradually and naturally.**

**Most common mistakes and how to avoid them:**

**Mistake 1: Getting 500 backlinks in the first month**
This looks unnatural to Google. 500 links to a brand new site in one month raises red flags. Instead, aim for 5–10 in month one, 10–20 in month two, 20–30 in month three — showing a natural growth curve.

**Mistake 2: Getting only one type of backlink**
If all your backlinks come only from guest posts or only from directories — there's no diversification. Google expects a natural backlink profile from various sources: blogs, news, directories, forums, social media.

**Mistake 3: Over-optimizing anchor text**
If all your backlinks use the same anchor text (e.g., "best SEO service Tashkent") — Google flags this as manipulation. A natural profile has diverse anchors: brand name, URL, "click here," and keyword-based anchors.

**Mistake 4: Focusing only on content without backlinks**
"Great content will earn its own backlinks" is partially true — but especially for new sites, this rarely works. A new site has no audience to discover the content. That's why active backlink outreach is necessary.

**Mistake 5: Buying backlinks from low-quality sites**
Services offering 1,000 backlinks for $5 are harmful to your site. Google's SpamBrain algorithm quickly detects such links and penalizes your site. Recovering from a penalty can take months.

In our experience, the most effective approach: first 2 months focus only on content, from month 3 start gradual outreach, and after 6 months launch an active backlink campaign. This creates a natural growth curve that looks trustworthy to Google.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Backlinks

**1. How many months should a site be active before getting backlinks?**
At least 3–6 months. But you can start your backlink strategy from day one — through content preparation and relationship building. Google may take 6–12 months to fully account for links.

**2. What age domains does Google consider trustworthy?**
Generally, domains over 1 year build baseline trust. 2–3 year domains have medium trust. 5+ year active domains have the highest trust.

**3. How many backlinks does a new site need?**
Quality over quantity. For a new site, 50–100 quality backlinks in the first 6 months is sufficient. 10–20 natural links per month is a good pace.

**4. Is buying backlinks risky?**
Yes. Google considers buying backlinks a Link Scheme and penalizes it. Only natural, editorial backlinks are safe and effective.

**5. Are nofollow backlinks useful?**
No direct ranking impact, but they bring traffic and make your backlink profile look natural. A healthy profile should have a mix of dofollow and nofollow.

**6. Can I copy my competitor's backlinks?**
Yes, you can analyze competitors' backlink profiles using Ahrefs or SEMrush and try to acquire links from the same sites. This is a legal and effective strategy.

**7. One strong backlink or 100 average ones — which is better?**
One strong backlink. A single editorial backlink from a DR 70+ site has more impact than 100 links from DR 5–10 sites.
