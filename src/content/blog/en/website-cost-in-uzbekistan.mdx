## What Affects Website Pricing — 6 Key Factors

To answer how much a website costs, you first need to understand what shapes the price. Here are 6 decisive factors:

### 1. Website Complexity and Number of Pages
The most fundamental factor is how complex the site is and how many pages it contains. The price difference between a one-page landing and a 50-page corporate site can be 5–10x. As pages increase, design, development, and content work grows proportionally.

### 2. Design — Template or Custom?
Building from a template is the cheapest option. But custom design strengthens brand identity and stands out from competitors. Price difference: template-based site $100–300, full custom design starts at $500+.

### 3. Functionality and Integrations
- Simple informational website
- CRM system integration (e.g., AmoCRM)
- Payment systems (Click, Payme, Stripe)
- Multilingual interface (i18n)
- Admin panel and management system
- API and third-party service connections

Each additional feature means additional work hours and corresponding cost.

### 4. Who Builds It: Agency, Freelancer, or In-House Team
An agency is typically more expensive, but the process is structured, responsibilities are clear, and support is guaranteed. A freelancer is cheaper, but project management often falls on the client. An in-house team is the most expensive option when you factor in salaries, tools, and time.

### 5. Technology Stack
Simple HTML/CSS, WordPress, or Tilda-based sites are cheaper. React, Next.js, custom backend, headless CMS — this is a professional stack with higher cost but also higher performance.

### 6. Project Timeline
Fast means expensive. If a client sets a 3-day deadline, agencies typically add an urgency surcharge. Standard timeline of 2–6 weeks keeps pricing normal.

## Landing Page Cost in Uzbekistan: When and For Whom?

A landing page is a single-purpose, single-page website. For selling a product, presenting a service, or lead generation — landing pages are the most effective tool.

Landing page pricing in Uzbekistan in 2026:
- **Basic (template):** $100–200
- **Mid-range (semi-custom):** $250–400
- **Professional (full custom design + animation):** $400–700+

Advantages of choosing a landing page:
- Fast to build (3–10 days)
- Affordable pricing
- Easy to A/B test
- Optimized for conversion

Disadvantage: Weaker for SEO, doesn't work well for multiple products/services.

> **Tip:** If you need a site for one service or one campaign — a landing page is the right choice. If you want to fully present your business — you need a corporate website.

## Corporate Website and Online Store: What You Need to Know

### Corporate Website

A corporate website fully represents a company with multiple sections. Typically: Home, About, Services, Portfolio, Blog, Contact.

**Corporate website pricing in Uzbekistan:**
- Minimal (5–7 pages, template): $300–600
- Mid-range (custom design, 7–15 pages): $700–1,500
- Large (20+ pages, with integrations): $1,500–3,000+

What affects the price? Design complexity, number of pages, admin panel, multilingual support.

### Online Store (E-commerce)

Online store pricing in Uzbekistan has the widest range — because functionality can vary greatly.

- **Simple catalog (10–50 products):** $500–1,000
- **Full e-commerce (payment, orders, personal account):** $1,200–3,000
- **Large platform (1,000+ products, API, warehouse):** $3,000–8,000+

E-commerce projects have many technical requirements: payment integration (Click, Payme), product catalog, cart, order management, admin panel. That's why the price is higher.

## Can You Get a Cheap Website? Ways to Save and Risks

Most people searching for "cheap website" choose one of two paths:

### 1. No-code/Website Builder Platforms
- **Tilda, Wix, Webflow** — allow quick and cheap website creation with ready templates
- Monthly payment: $10–50/month
- Disadvantage: limited flexibility, extra payment for custom domain, no code-level changes possible later

### 2. Cheap Freelancer or Student
- Price can drop to $50–150
- Risks: time delays, technical errors, no support, weak design

### Where You Can Save (Safely):
- Prepare content (text, photos) yourself — this is a separate cost for agencies
- Choose affordable but reliable hosting providers
- Start with minimal functionality, expand later

### Where Saving Is Risky:
- Design quality — this is the first impression
- Security (SSL, backup)
- Mobile responsiveness

> Remember: a low-quality website drives customers away. The $100 you saved won't cover the $1,000 in lost revenue.

## Website Creation Process: Step-by-Step Guide

The process from ordering a website to launching it typically consists of these stages:

**Stage 1: Briefing (1–2 days)**
You communicate goals, audience, functionality, and design requirements to the agency. A good agency comes with their own questionnaire.

**Stage 2: Proposal and Contract (2–3 days)**
The agency defines price, timeline, and scope of work. Contract is signed, initial payment is made (usually 30–50%).

**Stage 3: Design (5–14 days)**
Wireframe → UI Design → Approval. At this stage, you need to review and provide feedback. Don't delay.

**Stage 4: Development (7–21 days)**
Design is turned into code. Functionality is connected. Tests are conducted.

**Stage 5: Testing and Delivery (2–5 days)**
Checked on all devices and browsers. Content is placed. Domain is connected. Site goes live.

**Stage 6: Support**
A good agency provides 1–3 months of free support. After that, a monthly support contract can be arranged.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How much does it cost to build a website in Uzbekistan — average price?**
An average corporate website costs $500–1,500, a landing page $200–500. Price varies significantly based on project complexity and who builds it.

A simple landing page takes 5–10 days, a corporate site 3–6 weeks, an online store 4–10 weeks. Complex projects can take 2–3 months, which is normal.

In Uzbekistan, a domain (.uz) costs $10–30/year, hosting $5–20/month. International domains (.com) are in a similar range.

For small, simple projects, a freelancer may work. For corporate projects with strict deadlines and guarantees — an agency is more reliable.

Yes, website creation and SEO optimization are typically separate services. Basic SEO (fast loading, correct structure) is included by most agencies, but content SEO and link building are priced separately.

With platforms like Tilda and Wix, you can create a website even with minimal technical knowledge. But for a professional look and functionality, you need a specialist.
