## How a CRM System Works and What It Gives Your Business

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. At its core, a CRM system is software that centralizes every interaction between your business and its clients. But a modern CRM does far more than store phone numbers. Here is what a well-built CRM actually delivers:

**Unified client database.** Every contact, every conversation, every purchase — stored in one place. Any team member can pick up where another left off. No more lost spreadsheets or forgotten sticky notes.

**Sales pipeline management.** Visualize every deal from first contact to closed sale. Know exactly how many leads are at each stage, which deals are stuck, and where your revenue is coming from. Managers can forecast monthly targets with real data instead of guesswork.

**Task and workflow automation.** Automatically assign leads to sales reps based on region or product type. Send follow-up reminders, trigger email sequences after a meeting, or notify a manager when a deal exceeds a certain value. Automation eliminates repetitive manual work and reduces human error.

**Reports and analytics.** Real-time dashboards that show conversion rates, average deal size, sales cycle length, and team performance. Leadership gets visibility without asking for weekly reports — the data is always live.

**Integrations with other tools.** Connect your CRM to Telegram bots, payment systems (Click, Payme), accounting software (1C), email marketing platforms, and your website. Data flows between systems automatically, eliminating double entry and data silos.

In our experience, companies that implemented CRM saw sales conversion increase by 25–40% on average. The reason is simple: when you stop losing leads and start following up consistently, more prospects turn into paying customers.

## Ready CRM vs Custom — Which One to Choose?

This is the most common question businesses face. Platforms like Bitrix24, AmoCRM, and HubSpot offer ready-made CRM solutions that you can start using within days. Custom CRM development takes longer but delivers a system built specifically for your workflows. Let us compare them honestly.

**Ready-made CRM platforms** are ideal when your business processes are relatively standard. If you run a typical sales operation — leads come in, managers call them, deals close — a platform like AmoCRM will cover 80% of your needs out of the box. The setup is fast, the cost is predictable (monthly subscription), and you get regular updates from the vendor.

However, ready-made platforms come with limitations. You are locked into someone else's data structure. Customization is possible but often shallow. As your business grows or your processes become more specific, you start hitting walls — features you cannot change, integrations that do not exist, and monthly fees that keep climbing as you add users and modules.

**Custom CRM development** makes sense when your business has unique workflows that do not fit neatly into a generic platform. Perhaps you run a logistics company that needs route optimization integrated into the sales pipeline. Maybe you operate a clinic where patient records, appointment scheduling, and billing must live in one system. Or you need deep integration with Uzbekistan-specific payment systems and government reporting formats.

Here is a direct comparison:

| Criteria | Ready CRM | Custom CRM |
|---|---|---|
| Initial cost | $0–$200/month per user | $1,000–$15,000+ one-time |
| Launch time | 1–7 days | 2–6 months |
| Flexibility | Limited to platform features | Unlimited — built for your needs |
| Data control | Stored on vendor servers | Stored on your own servers |
| Long-term cost | Grows with users and modules | Fixed after development; maintenance only |

The bottom line: if your budget is tight and your processes are standard, start with a ready-made CRM. If you have specific requirements, need full data ownership, or want a system that scales exactly how your business scales — invest in custom development.

### 5 Factors That Determine CRM Price

1. **Number of modules.** Each module — contacts, deals, tasks, invoices, reports — requires separate design and development. More modules mean more hours and higher cost.

2. **Integration complexity.** Connecting to Telegram is relatively simple. Integrating with 1C accounting, government tax APIs, or custom ERP systems requires significantly more development time.

3. **Design requirements.** A functional admin-panel interface costs less than a polished, branded UI with custom dashboards and mobile-responsive layouts.

4. **User roles and permissions.** A system where everyone sees everything is simpler than one with granular role-based access control — sales reps see their leads, managers see their team, directors see company-wide analytics.

5. **Data migration.** If you are moving from Excel, another CRM, or a legacy database, data cleaning and migration adds time and cost to the project.

## CRM Development Process — From Start to Finish

Understanding the development process helps you plan timelines, budget, and internal resources. Here is how a professional CRM project unfolds, step by step.

### Step 1: Business Analysis (3–7 days)

Before writing a single line of code, we study your business. How does your sales process work today? What tools are you currently using? Where are the bottlenecks? Who will use the CRM and what do they need from it? This phase produces a clear understanding of requirements and priorities.

### Step 2: Technical Specification and Prototype (1–2 weeks)

Based on the analysis, we create a detailed technical specification — a document that describes every feature, every screen, and every integration. Alongside this, we build interactive prototypes (wireframes) so you can see and click through the system before development begins. This is where misunderstandings get caught early, saving significant time and money later.

### Step 3: UI/UX Design (1–2 weeks)

The prototype turns into a polished design. Our designers create interfaces that are not just attractive but practical — optimized for daily use by your team. Good CRM design means fewer clicks to complete common tasks, clear visual hierarchy, and an interface that new employees can learn in hours, not weeks.

### Step 4: Development (4–12 weeks)

This is the core phase. Frontend and backend developers build the system according to the technical specification and approved design. We work in sprints — short development cycles with regular demos — so you see progress every 1–2 weeks and can provide feedback before the project is too far along to change direction.

### Step 5: Testing and Launch (1–2 weeks)

Before launch, the system goes through thorough testing: functional testing (does everything work as specified?), integration testing (do all connected systems communicate correctly?), load testing (can it handle your expected number of users?), and user acceptance testing (does your team confirm it meets their needs?). After testing, we deploy to production and assist with the initial data migration.

### Step 6: Support and Maintenance (ongoing)

A CRM is not a static product. Your business evolves, and your system should evolve with it. Post-launch support includes bug fixes, minor improvements, server monitoring, and periodic updates. Many of our clients also return for new feature development as their business grows.

## How to Choose a CRM Development Company

The Uzbekistan market for software development is growing rapidly, which means you have more options — but also more risk of choosing the wrong partner. Here is how to evaluate potential CRM developers.

### Signs of a Reliable Development Company

1. **They ask questions before quoting a price.** A company that gives you a number in the first conversation without understanding your business is guessing — or worse, planning to cut corners.

2. **They show relevant portfolio work.** Not just websites or mobile apps, but actual CRM or business management systems they have built. Ask for case studies or demos.

3. **They have a structured process.** Look for evidence of business analysis, prototyping, milestone-based development, and testing. A professional team does not jump straight to coding.

4. **They discuss post-launch support upfront.** Building a CRM is only half the job. A reliable partner plans for ongoing maintenance, updates, and scaling from the beginning.

5. **They communicate clearly and consistently.** During the sales process, pay attention to response times, clarity of explanations, and willingness to answer difficult questions. This is a preview of what the development process will look like.

### Warning Signs to Watch For

1. **Unrealistically low prices.** If someone offers a full CRM system for $300, they are either using a basic template with minimal customization or plan to charge heavily for changes later.

2. **No written specification.** A developer who starts coding without a documented technical specification is building on sand. Changes and misunderstandings will cost you dearly.

3. **No testing phase.** If "testing" is not mentioned in the project plan, expect bugs in production and frustrated employees.

4. **Vague timelines.** "It will be ready when it is ready" is not a project plan. Professional teams commit to milestones with clear deliverables at each stage.

A simple CRM can be built in 2–3 months. A medium-complexity system typically takes 3–4 months.
